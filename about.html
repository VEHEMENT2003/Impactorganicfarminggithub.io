About the Internship

Our internship aimed to promote the benefits and practices of organic farming. Our team of five students worked together to increase awareness and understanding of sustainable agriculture practices among farmers and the community.

Key Learnings

  • Importance of Sustainable Practices: Organic farming conserves the environment and improves soil quality, leading to higher yields during adverse conditions like droughts.
  • Economic and Health Benefits: Reducing the use of chemical fertilizers lowers production costs and minimizes health hazards for both humans and animals.
  • Community Engagement: Continuous education and support are crucial for encouraging farmers to transition to organic farming.

Challenges Faced by Farmers

Despite its benefits, organic farming faces several challenges. Farmers often resort to chemical fertilizers due to the quick release of nutrients and higher immediate yields. Organic fertilizers, on the other hand, require more time and knowledge to produce results. High costs, lack of awareness, and the need for more immediate production often deter farmers from adopting organic practices.

Conversations with Stakeholders

Interview with Mr. Som Kumar Chauhan, Chairman of IMC

Interviewer: Mr. Chauhan, what are the main organic fertilizers your company offers, and how are they being received by the farming community?

Mr. Chauhan: We have two primary products, Agro Activator and Growth Booster, which are both affordable and effective. Farmers have reported good results, and these products help them reduce reliance on chemical fertilizers.

Discussion with Elizabeth Dakhar, Department of Agriculture and Horticulture, Meghalaya

Interviewer: Why do farmers in the region still prefer chemical fertilizers over organic options?

Elizabeth Dakhar: Chemical fertilizers provide quick growth and are cost-effective in the short term. However, they degrade soil health over time. We are working on educating farmers about the long-term benefits of organic farming.

Interaction with Pro. Pra. Uttreshawar Surve, Agriculture Officer

Interviewer: Professor Surve, why do farmers continue to use chemical fertilizers despite the known disadvantages?

Pro. Surve: Organic farming is time-consuming, and farmers see more immediate profit with chemical fertilizers. The strength of crops is increased with chemicals, leading to higher yields.

Interviewer: What are the disadvantages of chemical farming?

Pro. Surve: Chemical farming mainly leads to loss of soil fertility, decreases in crop production over time, and the presence of harmful chemicals in fruits and vegetables, which can cause diseases like cancer.

Interviewer: Can you give an example?

Pro. Surve: Take wheat, for instance. Using chemical fertilizers, a farmer can produce 50 to 60 quintals, but with organic fertilizers, it drops to about 10 to 20 quintals. After harvesting the first crop with chemicals, farmers often double the amount of chemical fertilizers for the next crop to maintain high production. For organic farming to be widely adopted, there need to be more companies offering organic products.

Interview with a Local Farmer, Mr. Pradip Biswas

Interviewer: Mr. Biswas, what challenges do you face with organic farming, and how have you tried to overcome them?

Mr. Biswas: The main challenge is the high cost of organic fertilizers and pest control. I mix regular fertilizers with cow dung and use chemical pesticides sparingly. However, with the introduction of IMC's products, I'm considering a full transition to organic methods.

Conversation with Mr. Jayanta Layek, Scientist at ICAR

Interviewer: Mr. Layek, how has ICAR's Integrated Organic Farming System (IOFS) model impacted local farmers?

Mr. Layek: The IOFS model has significantly increased farmers' incomes by diversifying their agricultural practices. It also promotes sustainable farming by integrating different farming components, which enhances soil fertility and crop yields.

Interventions and Solutions

We implemented several interventions to support farmers in transitioning to organic farming:

  • Awareness and Education: Engaged with farmers to educate them about the long-term benefits of organic farming.
  • Product Demonstration: Collaborated with IMC to introduce farmers to affordable organic products like Agro Activator and Growth Booster.
  • Government and Institutional Support: Approached the Department of Agriculture and Horticulture in Meghalaya and ICAR at Umian, Meghalaya.
  • Practical Solutions: Suggested crop rotation practices and connected farmers to local businesses for organic compost.

Conclusion

Our internship journey in promoting organic farming has been enlightening and rewarding. Through collaborative efforts and dedication, we've gained valuable insights into the challenges faced by farmers and the potential of sustainable agriculture practices.

Despite the prevalent use of chemical fertilizers, our interactions with stakeholders, including farmers, agriculture officers, and industry experts, have highlighted the growing awareness and interest in organic alternatives. While there are hurdles to overcome, such as cost and knowledge barriers, the benefits of organic farming in terms of soil health, crop quality, and environmental sustainability are undeniable.

As a team, we remain committed to advocating for the widespread adoption of organic farming practices. By continuing to engage with farmers, supporting research and innovation, and fostering partnerships with government agencies and private organizations, we aim to contribute to a more sustainable and resilient agricultural future.

Together, we believe that every small step towards organic farming not only enhances food security and livelihoods but also protects the planet for future generations.
